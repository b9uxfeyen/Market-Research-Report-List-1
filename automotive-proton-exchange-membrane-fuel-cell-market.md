<p><p>The Automotive Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cell (PEMFC) market is characterized by three main types of hydrogen storage: compressed gaseous hydrogen, cryogenic liquid hydrogen, and hydrides. Compressed gaseous hydrogen is stored at high pressures in tanks, providing a practical solution for fueling vehicles. Cryogenic liquid hydrogen involves storing hydrogen at extremely low temperatures, which increases energy density. Hydrides utilize metal alloys to store hydrogen in solid form, offering a safe and compact storage option ideal for automotive applications.</p></p>
